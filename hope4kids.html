PROJECT

REDESIGNING HOPE-4-KIDS website FOR BETTER ENGAGEMENT & donations

SUMMARY

The Hope-4-Kids website redesign focuses on creating a more engaging and visually cohesive platform to boost traffic, volunteers, and donations. The project aims to enhance user experience and support Hope-4-Kids' mission more effectively.

RESPONSIBILITIES

  • UI Design
  • UX Design
  • Art Direction
  • Information Architecture

STARTING POINT

At the heart of our journey with Hope-4-Kids.org lies a commitment to enhancing every digital interaction for our community. Our goal? To transform the user interface and experience, unlocking new pathways for engagement, volunteerism, and support. By addressing key design challenges—from visual coherence to intuitive navigation—we're paving the way for a more impactful online presence. Join us as we embark on a collaborative journey, blending creativity with strategy to amplify Hope-4-Kids.org's mission and reach.

BEFORE WE BEGIN

DEFINING THE SCOPE

Defining the scope of a project is a crucial skill for any UX/UI designer, ensuring that the project stays on track and meets client expectations. For this travel app project, we initiated the collaboration with a Zoom meeting, where we thoroughly discussed and identified the client's specific needs. This initial meeting allowed me to estimate the time and resources required to develop an effective solution. We continued our communication via Slack, allowing us to quickly address any questions and align on details. Once the scope was clearly defined and agreed upon, we moved forward to the exciting phase of design exploration.

PHASE 1 UX RESEARCH

IDENTIFY NEEDS, EXPLORE SOLUTIONS

First, budget management emerges as a significant concern for travelers, indicating a need for features or services within the travel app that facilitate cost-effective planning while ensuring an enjoyable experience.

Second, the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic is evident in users' heightened attention to safety guidelines and travel budgeting, suggesting the importance of integrating real-time updates and relevant information regarding price updates into the app.

Third, the preference for traveling during non-peak seasons to avoid crowds and secure better prices underscores the significance of offering flexible scheduling options and providing insights into off-peak travel opportunities.

Overall, We chose to focus on the pervasive issue of overspending among young travelers by providing a platform that simplifies budget management during trips. By understanding the unique needs and preferences of young travelers, we seek to develop a platform that not only helps users set and track their budgets but also provides tailored recommendations and insights to enhance their overall travel experience. Through innovative features and a focus on user engagement, we aim to become the go-to solution for young travelers seeking to make the most of their budgets while exploring the world.

DEFINING THE USER

Through user interviews, we gathered valuable insights into the needs and preferences of our target audience. These conversations revealed key concerns such as budget management, safety guidelines, and a preference for non-peak travel. By analyzing this data, we were able to create a detailed user persona that embodies the typical young traveler. This persona will guide our design decisions and ensure that the app effectively addresses the real-world challenges faced by our users, ultimately helping us build a more user-centric and engaging travel app.

PHASE 2

REFINE THE MOBILE EXPERIENCE

In refining the mobile experience, I led the team in defining a user flow that streamlined the travel planning process. We utilized a feature prioritization matrix activity to identify key functionalities and conducted an "I Like, I Wish, What If" activity to gather feedback and brainstorm improvements. Due to time constraints, we focused on two main features: the Budget Builder, which helps users manage their travel expenses effectively, and the Booking Rentals feature, allowing users to book accommodations at varying prices based on the day and time. These prioritized features ensure a seamless and user-centric experience.

PHASE 3

LOOKS ARE IMPORTANT TOO

The UI process was pivotal in creating an appealing and intuitive interface for our travel app. We employed A/B testing and user testing to refine the design, ensuring it met user expectations and enhanced usability. Through these iterative tests, we arrived at a solid UI that incorporates a design technique called "neumorphism." This approach blends elements of skeuomorphism and flat design, creating a soft, realistic aesthetic that is both modern and user-friendly. The result is a visually engaging interface that enhances the overall user experience.

IN CONCLUSION

I've discovered the importance of understanding the intricate user journey within an application. Every minute detail plays a crucial role in shaping the user experience. Crafting a prototype was a fascinating endeavor with trying to come up with new and fresh designs. Furthermore, ensuring smooth and seamless functionality added another layer of complexity. Despite the challenges, I found immense satisfaction in the development process, from the initial stages of user research to the culmination in a polished digital prototype. This experience has not only expanded my understanding of user-centric design but has also instilled in me a deep appreciation for the intricacies of application development.
